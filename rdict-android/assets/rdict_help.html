RDict - Help

New to RDict?

If you are new to RDict and want to get an idea of how it works, read on.  We'll give you a quick tour of how RDict can be used to look up the definitions of words, and to help maintain and improve your vocabulary.

Setup

Before RDict can be used, you have to do a quick setup process.  If you are able to view this help page, then you've already successfully installed RDict and downloaded the database file.  So we can skip over that part here.  Let's start by describing the basic idea behind RDict.

The Basic Idea

Everybody would benefit from a better vocabulary.  RDict aims to make learning new words more convenient, and help you remember the new words you come across.  To do so, RDict makes use of two main components: a Dictionary and Flashcard Quizzes.  The dictionary allows you to look up the definitions of new words you come across conveniently.  The flashcard quizzes give you an effective way to practice the words you look up.

Studying with RDict works in three steps.  When you encounter a word you don't know, you

  1. Look up the word in the dictionary.
  2. Browse the dictionary page and tell RDict which definition you want help with remembering.
  3. Do flashcard quizzes prepared by RDict to remember the words you look up.

The rest of this help file describes each of the three steps above in more detail.

The Four Tabs

When setup finishes, RDict shows you four tabs at the bottom of the screen.  Each tab gives you access to different features:
  • The Search Tab: This is where you search for and look up words.  In this tab, you can also choose definitions to get help with remembering.
  • The Review Tab: This is where you do your daily vocabulary review.  This screen allows you to start flashcard quizzes.  You can also view statistics on your flashcards and on your study performance.
  • The History Tab:  The History tab provides you with easy access to past dictionary searches.
  • The More Tab: In addition to the Help file you are viewing now, the More tab also allows you to view licensing information, manage your flashcard stack, and adjust default settings.
In the next three sections, we describe each tab in more detail.

The Search Tab

After Setup, you will see the Search screen.  The Search screen is the dictionary part of RDict where you can look up the definition of words; this screen is also the place where you tell RDict which words you want help remembering.  

When RDict first loads, it always displays the Search tab first.  The picture below shows the Search screen just after Setup.

Searching

Looking up a word on the Search screen is simple: touch the search box and begin typing your word.  You can type the whole word, or type the first few letters and then browse to your word by scrolling down the list.

*middle of a search pic*

If your word doesn't appear in the list, that means RDict doesn't have a definition for that word.

Viewing Definitions

To choose a word, simply touch it, and RDict will display the dictionary page for the word.

Dictionary pages in RDict look like the picture shown below.  They are a slightly modified version of the online Wiktionary pages.

*dictionary page sample*

The word you looked up appears first, followed by its etymology, definitions, and other information. The structure of the entry resembles the structure in the original Wiktionary web page. A separate entry exists for the noun, verb or other parts of speech. Synonyms are often provided. In some cases, examples are provided too.

Shortcuts

In the dictionary page for a word, some words will appear in blue. Blue words are a convenient shortcut: simply touch them and RDict will show you the definition for the word.  No typing required.

Backwards and Forwards Navigation

At times, you may want to compare the definitions of two words, or simply review a definition you looked up previously, after using a shortcut.  For your convenience, dictionary pages in RDict have 'Backwards' and 'Forwards' arrows at the top of the screen.  These arrows are just like the buttons on your web browser.

Asking RDict for help with remembering a definition

You've probably already noticed the star buttons on the dictionary screen (** icon here**). Their purpose is as follows: if, after looking up a word, you decide that this is a word you'd like help with remembering, use the star button.

To use the star button, review the definitions in the dictionary page, and press the star beside the definition you would like to remember.  When you press the star button, RDict creates a new flashcard for the word and adds it to your card collection.  After you press the star button, RDict shows you a brief message to confirm that the definition was added.

The Review Tab

In RDict, you maintain and improve your vocabulary by doing Flashcard Quizzes.  RDict quizzes you on the words you've selected for review with the star button.  Let's look at reviewing in more detail.

Review Home Screen

Pressing the review tab brings you to the Review Home screen. The Review Home screen has two main sections: the Exercise section (the top half of the screen), and the Study Statistics section (the bottom half).

** picture here**

Here's a description:
  • Review Exercise Section: From this area, you can start Flashcard Quizzes.  On the left, you will see a brief description of the quiz.  On the right, there is a 'Go!' button you can press to start an exercise.
  • Study Statistics Section: This area gives you information about your Flashcard Collection and how well you remember the cards in your collection. 

We'll begin with the Review Exercises first.

Review Exercises

RDict provides two review exercises: Today's Scheduled Flashcards and Words Looked up Today.

Today's Scheduled Flashcards

Scheduled cards is the main way of using RDict.  As you lookup more words, your flashcard collection grows.  After some time, it becomes impractical to practice all the cards in your collection; you only have time to review a limited number of cards.

How to choose cards for review?  One way might be to do choose cards from your collection randomly.  Another way might be to select cards most recently added to your stack.  A better way might be to chooses cards based on how easy it is for you to remember the word and when you are likely to forget the definition; this is how RDict works.

When you use a star button to create a flashcard, RDict schedules a practice time for you to view that card. The first practice time is usually the same day you press the star button.  Future practice times depend on how easy or hard it is for you to recall the definition of the word on the card.  Difficult cards are scheduled for review in the near future; easier cards are scheduled for review farther in the future.  And, as cards become easier, they are scheduled farther into the future.  

In the Today's Scheduled Flashcards quiz,  you practice cards that are scheduled for today.

Sometimes there are cards scheduled for today, and sometimes not.  It's easy to find out if there are.  Take a look at the screenshot of the tab bar shown below.

*screen pic*

See the little '12' number on the 'Review' tab?  That number is telling you that there are 12 cards scheduled for practice today.  The number is updated each day, according to how many cards are scheduled for practice.  

There is also another type of review exercise called Looked up Today.  In this exercise, you practice the cards that you have selected for review with the star button today.

Next, let's look at how a flashcard quiz actually works.

Flashcard Quizzes

Flashcard Quizzes in RDict are straightforward. When you click the 'Go!' button to start a quiz, RDict loads today's scheduled cards and shows you each card, one at a time.

First, you'll see the front of the card. The front will have a word on it, like in the example below.

*front of flashcard pic*

By showing you the front of a card, RDict is challenging you to remember the definition. Try to remember it.  Once you've remembered the definition (or realized you've forgotten it), press 'View Answer'. This will flip the card over to the other side, revealing the definition of the word (shown below).

*back of flashcard pic*

Did you remember the definition correctly? Was it easy? Or did you forget? RDict wants to know! Tell RDict how easy or hard it was for you to remember the definition by using one of the five buttons that appear on the bottom of the card. For example, if you have totally forgotten the definition, press the 'I forget' button.

After you press one of the buttons, RDict will show you the next card to review.  When you finish the last card, RDict returns you to the Review Home screen.

Study Statistics Description

Sometimes it's nice to know how well your studying is going.  In RDict, we've tried to give you some feedback on your studying by showing you a couple of simple statistics that we think might be useful for you when you study.

The first statistic is about your flashcard collection.  In text, we show you how many cards you have in your stack, and how many cards you have added today.  Next to the text, we have a line graph; the line graph shows you how your total number of cards has grown over the past 30 days.

The second statistic is a score representing how well you know the cards in your collection.  The score is a percentage, with 100% being the maximum score and 0% the minimum.  

RDict calculates your score in the following way.  First, it calculates the average of each individual word score over the last three quizzes.  We call this the card score.  The overall score is the average card score, for all the cards in your collection.  To get the maximum, score, you have to be able to easily recall all the words in your flashcard collection three consecutive times.  Next to the text, we have a second line graph which shows you how your score has changed over the past 30 days.

Summary

And that's it for reviewing!  Use RDict once a day and check the Review tab to see if there are cards scheduled for today.  If so, practice the words with a flashcard quiz.  Check the card statistics to watch your progress in learning the words you look up with the dictionary.

Other Tabs

The Search and Review tabs in RDict are the main tabs where you'll do most of your work, but there are others.  Weve included a History tab and a More tab to make using RDict more convenient and useful.

The History Tab

There may be times when you would like quick access to words you've looked up in the past.  For example, you may wish to view the full definition of a word, and not only the smaller section included on a flashcard.

To make looking up past words more convenient, we've included a History tab which gives you easy access to past searches.

Each word you look up is shown in a list, organized by date.  Words looked up no the same day appear in the order they were looked up.  Here's a picture!

*History tab*

To view the full definition for a word, scroll through the list untill you find the word and then touch the row.  RDict will load the full dictionary page, which is identical to the page you viewed in the Search tab.

The More Tab

The more tab is where we've put features that might come in handy every now and then, but that you probably won't be using as often as the Search, Review and History features.

In addition to this help file you are viewing now, the More tab gives you access to 3 other features in RDict.

About

Ok, so this isn't really a feature, and many eople might never look at it, but the About screen does provide you with information on the RDict version number and license, as well as the license for the WIktionary data we use and for the third party software components used to develop RDict.

Manage

We've included a Manage screen to allow you to control your flashcard collection.  Like the description located to the left of the button says, from this screen you can Add cards, or Edit and Delete cards in your collection.  Pressing the Manage button launches the Card Manager screen, which looks like the following:

* care manager screen*

Editing and Deleting cards is accomplished in two steps.

First, browse to the card you want to Edit or Delete by scrolling through the card list until you see the card you're after.  If your collection is very big, or you prefer typing, you can use the text box at the top to filter the cards in the list.  Type the first few letters of the word to speed up your search, just like in the searching part of the Search tab.

Stop scrolling when you see the card you want, and press the button to the right of the card to go to the Edit screen.

The Edit Screen should resemble something like the screen below.

* picture of card edit screen here*

Using the Edit screen is straightforward:
  • Edit the definition and press 'Save' to save your changes.
  • If you want to delete the card from your collection, use the Delete button.
  • Cancel your changes with the Cancel button.
Finally, if you'd like to create a new card for a word that isn't included in RDict, use the New Card button located to the right of the text box at the top.  On the Add screen, enter the word and definition to create a card and add it to your collection.
